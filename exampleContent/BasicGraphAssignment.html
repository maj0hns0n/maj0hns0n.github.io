Do opioid overdoes tend to be associated with less affluent areas—that is, areas where families have lower incomes?¶

Yes, there appears to be a negative relationship between families with lower incomes and higher opioid overdoses. I chose a scatterplot to examine the relationship between the two numeric variables (MedianHouseholdIncome and FPOO-Rate) since it clearly identifies a cluster of data points in the lower-end of the income scale associated with higher overdose rates.

In [92]:
fig, ax = plt.subplots()

ax.scatter(df_op['MedianHouseholdIncome'], df_op['FPOO-Rate'], alpha=0.5, c = 'g')  
fig.suptitle('Median Income vs. Opioid Overdose Rate ')   
ax.xaxis.set_label_text('Median Household Income')  
ax.yaxis.set_label_text('Opioid Overdose Rate')       
fig.set_size_inches(7,5)
plt.show()
